No.4 Commando

ArmA3 WW2 Relaxed MilSim Unit

About the unit

Who were the real No.4 Commandos?

The British Commandos were formed during the Second World War in June 1940, following a request from the Prime Minister of the United Kingdom, Winston Churchill, for a force that could carry out hard hitting attacks on German positions on any front. No. 4 Commando was a battalion-sized unit and although it was raised to conduct small-scale raids and harass garrisons along the coast of German occupied France, it was mainly employed as a highly trained infantry assault unit.

What is this unit about?

Training in the Scottish Highlands to beach landings in Normandy to Norway, ambushing enemies using iconic gear, weapons and vehicles provided by the mods we use as well as our own exclusive content. Sniper nests in clock towers to concrete pillbox's on open fields with para-drops into flak filled skies. The missions and scenarios are endless and we aim to replicate the conflict as closely to real life as possible whilst also keeping the relaxed aspect.

What makes us stick out from all the other units?

We take great pride to define ourselves as the old-school people. The ones that prefer to see the enemy directly through the iron sight of our bolt-action rifles, rather than through some kind of high-power magnfication lens attached to an automatic rifle. We face Death from up close on the battlefield and yet its threat and menace finds and shall always find us unafraid. Doesn't matter how dirty, how dangerous the business, we do it and we do it well.

    • The arsenal

    (Click on the images for additional information)

    The SMLE No.4 Mk. I

    This is the standard issue rifle given to all new Recruits as well as riflemen, it fires a .303 bullet and has an internal 10-round magazine that is reloaded via stripper clip. The effective range of the weapon is roughly 500m.

    The SMLE No. 4 Mk. I (T)

    This rifle is identical to the SMLE No. 4 Mk. I in every way except this variant is only issued to marksmen and is fitted with a No. 32 3x telescopic sight for better accuracy at range. This extends the effective range to upwards of 700m with a skilled shooter.

    The Sten Mk. II

    The Sten Mk. II is the standard issue sub-machine gun of the British Expeditionary Force. It fires a 9mm Parabellum cartridge and has a unique side-mounted 32-round detachable box magazine. Effective range is roughly 200m.

    The Sten Mk. II (S)

    The suppressed version of the Sten Mk. II used primarily for stealth operations but sometime also carried by Commandos during regular operations.

    The Sten Mk. V

    The Sten Mk. V is the paratrooper variant of the Sten Gun and is carried by some Commandos during, you guessed it, airborne operations.

    The Welrod Mk. II

    The Welrod is an exclusively special operations weapon used for silently assassinating enemy soldiers and sentries. It features a 6-round .45 ACP magazine which also acts as the pistol grip. Effective range is 10m at most.

    The M1 Bazooka

    The M1 Bazooka is used by the Commandos to fight off armored vehicles or tanks but can also be used to destroy strategic buildings and alike. It fires a 60mm M6 rocket. Effective range is 200m, after this range the rocket is still lethal however not anymore accurate.

    The Bren Mk. II

    The Bren Mk. II is a light machine gun that can deliver continuos fire without overheating as fast. It, like the SMLE, fires a .303 cartridge and is fed by a unique top-mounted 30-round detachable box magazine. Effective range is 500m.
